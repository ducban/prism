# Opportunity Solution Tree

## Overview

**English:**
The Opportunity Solution Tree (OST) is a visual framework created by Teresa Torres in 2016 that helps product teams connect desired business outcomes to customer opportunities, potential solutions, and experiments. It's especially powerful when handling stakeholder-driven requests because it forces you to validate assumptions before building.

**When to use it:**
- CEO/stakeholder requests a specific feature (e.g., "Add AI section to homepage")
- Need to explore problem space before jumping to solutions
- Want to ensure you're solving the right problem
- Multiple potential solutions exist for a business goal

## üìã Opportunity Solution Tree Playbook

### Structure of the Tree

```
                    Desired Outcome
                          |
        __________________|__________________
       |                  |                  |
   Opportunity 1     Opportunity 2      Opportunity 3
       |                  |                  |
    ___|___            ___|___            ___|___
   |   |   |          |   |   |          |   |   |
  S1  S2  S3         S4  S5  S6         S7  S8  S9
  |   |   |          |   |   |          |   |   |
  E   E   E          E   E   E          E   E   E

Legend:
S = Solution (potential features/products)
E = Experiment (test to validate solution)
```

**The 4 Levels Explained:**

1. **Desired Outcome (Root)**: Business goal you want to achieve
   - Must be specific and measurable
   - Example: "Increase trial-to-paid conversion from 12% to 20% by Q2"

2. **Opportunities (Level 1)**: Customer needs, pain points, or desires
   - Discovered through user research, interviews, data
   - Example: "Users don't understand our value in first 24 hours"

3. **Solutions (Level 2)**: Potential features or products to address opportunities
   - Multiple solutions per opportunity
   - Example: "Interactive onboarding tour" or "AI-powered setup wizard"

4. **Experiments (Level 3)**: Tests to validate if solution will work
   - Must be quick and cheap to run
   - Example: "Figma prototype test with 10 users"

---

### Step-by-Step Process

**Step 1: Define Your Desired Outcome**

Start with a clear, measurable business goal.

**Good Examples:**
- "Increase user retention from 40% to 60% in 6 months"
- "Reduce support tickets by 30% this quarter"
- "Grow monthly revenue per user from $50 to $75"

**Bad Examples:**
- "Make users happy" (not measurable)
- "Improve the product" (too vague)
- "Add more features" (output, not outcome)

**For Root:**
If CEO says "Add AI section to homepage", first ask: **What outcome are we trying to achieve?**
- Increase sign-ups?
- Improve brand perception?
- Reduce "What is this?" support questions?

---

**Step 2: Discover Opportunities (Customer Needs)**

Research to find WHY customers aren't achieving your desired outcome.

**Discovery Methods:**
- **User interviews**: "Tell me about the last time you tried to..."
- **Analytics**: Where do users drop off?
- **Support tickets**: What problems do they report?
- **Surveys**: "What prevents you from..."

**For "Add AI section" request:**

Instead of immediately building it, research:
- Opportunity 1: "Visitors don't understand how our product uses AI"
- Opportunity 2: "Technical users want to know AI capabilities before signing up"
- Opportunity 3: "Sales team needs AI messaging to close enterprise deals"

Notice: Each opportunity represents a different user need. The CEO's request might solve one, some, or none of these!

---

**Step 3: Generate Multiple Solutions**

For EACH opportunity, brainstorm 3-5 potential solutions.

**Example for Opportunity 1** ("Visitors don't understand how we use AI"):

Solution ideas:
- Add "AI-Powered" badges next to relevant features
- Create explainer video on homepage
- Add AI section with technical details
- Live demo showing AI in action
- Comparison chart: "With AI vs Without AI"

**Why multiple solutions matter:**
The CEO's request ("Add AI section") is just ONE possible solution. Maybe the live demo would work better? You don't know until you test.

---

**Step 4: Design Experiments**

For each solution, create a quick test BEFORE building it fully.

**Experiment Guidelines:**
- ‚è±Ô∏è **Fast**: Should take days, not weeks
- üí∞ **Cheap**: Minimal engineering effort
- üìä **Measurable**: Clear success criteria
- üéØ **Focused**: Test one assumption

**Example Experiments:**

| Solution | Experiment | Success Metric | Time |
|----------|-----------|----------------|------|
| AI section on homepage | Add mockup + intercept 50 visitors for feedback | 70%+ say "Now I understand what you do" | 3 days |
| Live demo | Loom video on homepage for 1 week | 30%+ watch >50% of video | 1 week |
| AI badges | Add badges to 3 features, A/B test | 20%+ increase in trial sign-ups | 2 weeks |

**Result**: Now you have data to show CEO which solution actually works, not just gut feeling!

### Common Mistakes to Avoid

| Mistake | Why It's Bad | Better Approach |
|---------|-------------|-----------------|
| **Starting with solutions** | "CEO wants AI section, let's build it" | Start with outcome: "Why do we need this? What outcome are we driving?" |
| **One solution per opportunity** | Miss better alternatives | Generate 3-5 solutions per opportunity |
| **Skipping experiments** | Build wrong thing for 3 months | Test with prototype in 3 days |
| **Vague outcomes** | Can't measure success | Make outcomes specific and measurable |
| **Ignoring user research** | Solve problems users don't have | Validate opportunities with real users |

### Complete Example: CEO Requests "AI Section on Homepage"

**‚ùå Traditional Approach (Wrong):**
1. CEO: "Add AI section to homepage"
2. PM: "Sure, I'll have design mock it up"
3. 2 months later: Section launched
4. Result: No impact on metrics. CEO: "Why didn't this work?"

**‚úÖ OST Approach (Correct):**

**Step 1: Clarify Desired Outcome**
PM to CEO: "What outcome are we trying to achieve with the AI section?"
CEO: "We're losing deals because buyers think we're 'just another tool'. I want them to see we're AI-powered and innovative."

**Outcome**: Increase enterprise deal close rate from 15% to 25% in Q1

---

**Step 2: Discover Opportunities** (Research phase - 1 week)

Interviewed 10 lost prospects + 10 sales team members:

**Opportunities Found:**
1. **Opportunity A**: "Buyers can't articulate our differentiation to their leadership"
   - Evidence: 8/10 lost deals said "Couldn't explain why you vs. competitor"

2. **Opportunity B**: "Technical evaluators want AI architecture details"
   - Evidence: Sales team gets 5+ questions per demo about AI infrastructure

3. **Opportunity C**: "Procurement teams concerned about AI costs/risks"
   - Evidence: 6/10 deals stall at legal review due to AI compliance questions

---

**Step 3: Generate Solutions**

**For Opportunity A** (Can't articulate differentiation):
- Solution 1: AI section on homepage
- Solution 2: "Battle card" PDF for champions to share internally
- Solution 3: ROI calculator showing AI-powered efficiency gains
- Solution 4: Video: "How [Customer] closed deals faster with our AI"

**For Opportunity B** (Need technical details):
- Solution 5: Technical AI documentation page
- Solution 6: Architecture diagram in sales deck
- Solution 7: Live demo of AI features during trial

---

**Step 4: Design Experiments** (Week 2-3)

| Solution | Experiment | Duration | Cost |
|----------|-----------|----------|------|
| Battle card PDF | Create 1-pager, test with 5 champions | 3 days | Designer: 4 hours |
| ROI calculator | Figma prototype, walk through with 5 prospects | 5 days | PM: 8 hours |
| AI homepage section | Mockup + UserTesting.com with 20 target buyers | 1 week | $200 + Designer: 8 hours |

---

**Step 5: Run Experiments & Analyze**

**Results after 2 weeks:**

- **Battle card**: 5/5 champions said "This is exactly what I needed to present to my VP"
- **ROI calculator**: 4/5 prospects wanted to use it immediately, requested live version
- **AI homepage section**: 8/20 thought it was "nice but not critical"

**Decision**: Build battle card (2 days) + ROI calculator (1 week) FIRST. Deprioritize homepage section.

**Outcome**: After implementing:
- Deal close rate increased from 15% ‚Üí 28% (exceeded goal!)
- Time to close decreased by 12 days
- CEO: "This is why we validate before building!"

**Key Insight**: The CEO's requested solution (AI section) was NOT the best solution for the actual business outcome!
